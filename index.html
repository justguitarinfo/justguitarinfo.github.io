Guitar Plateau Breaker

Late beginning and intermediate-level guitar players can break their plateau by following a strategically crafted roadmap to becoming musically literate. Music literacy involves 4 things in my opinion: 1) the ability to read, 2) the ability to create, 3) the ability to perform, and 4) the ability to listen.

What does a plateau look like?

There are lots of varying symptoms.

  • Can play open-position (cowboy) chords but unsure why some chords go well with others.
  • Over-reliance on shapes and patterns. Tired of playing the pentatonic pattern because it's now becoming boring.
  • Don't really know how to utilize the full fretboard.
  • Yet to tackle "theory" - not sure where to start.
  • Would like to learn how to solo/improvise or would like to learn how to play more melodically.
  • General understanding of the instrument/fretboard is lacking.
  • Would like to piece together interesting chord progressions and put a melody over them.
  • Cannot identify basic chord progressions by ear.
  • A feeling of FRUSTRATION because you want to be a competent musician and make good music but don't know where to begin amongst the SEA of information online.
  • Looking for a roadmap that will get you from point A (late beginner/intermediate) to B (a competent musician that can express themselves).

If you want to get good, get really really really good at the fundamentals

The problem with beginners of all instruments is that they try to approximate what the experts are doing.

The truth is that there are levels to musicality. Experts are creating fantastic music at the highest level. But there are excellent musicians who are using slightly less complex concepts and STILL making great music. And the truth is that beginner musicians can make beautiful music by becoming experts at the fundamentals.

The fastest path to learning how to really enjoy playing your instrument is to find the way to maximize the musicality with the least amount of information as possible.

And the study of endless scales is not how you become a good musician!

We're going to focus on harmony instead. And the basic building block of harmony is the Triad.

It's time that you become a master of the triad

Mastering the triad will get you playing beautiful music much faster than trying to master scales and chord-scale relationships. The small irony is that as you master harmony, you'll create much better melodies.
